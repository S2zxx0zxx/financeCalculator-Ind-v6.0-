⏱ 1. Ek Saal Ki Roller Coaster — April 2025 Se Ab Tak

April 2, 2025 — Donald Trump ne "Liberation Day" pe poori duniya pe reciprocal tariffs announce kiye. India pe 26% tariff laga diya gaya. Iss announcement ke 5 din baad — April 7 ko — Nifty 5.9% gir gaya ek hi din mein. FII ne ek hafte mein ₹22,000 crore equity dump kar di. Rupee 87.5 tak pahunch gaya.

Phir August 2025 mein Russia se oil deal ke penalty ke saath India pe tariff 50% tak pahunch gaya — worst case scenario. Lekin kahaani yahan khatam nahi hui. February 2026 mein PM Modi aur President Trump ki call hui, framework sign hua, aur SCOTUS ne February 20 ko IEEPA tariff illegal declare kar diya. Ab India pe sirf 10% Section 122 surcharge hai.

📊 2. Abhi Exactly Kitna Tariff Lag Raha Hai India Pe?

Confusion hai toh clear karte hain. India pe alag-alag cheezein hain:

  • Section 122 Surcharge: 10% flat ALL goods pe (July 24, 2026 tak). Yeh MFN base rate ke upar add hota hai.
  • Section 232 (Steel/Aluminum): 25% — pehle se tha, abhi bhi hai.
  • Solar Panel CVD: 126% countervailing duty — India-made solar panels pe.
  • IT Services: ZERO — tariff services pe nahi lagta, sirf goods pe.
  • Pharma: Largely exempt — essential medicines global supply chain protect karne ke liye.

✅ 4. Kaunse Sectors Bachenge? — Safe Zone

Tariff war mein sab sectors equal nahi hain. IT aur pharma ne historically har global crisis ko outperform kiya hai. Domestic consumption plays bhi safe hain.

Safe Sectors

Low/Zero Tariff Risk
  • IT Services — Tariff lagta hi nahi services pe
  • Generic Pharma — Exempt list mein hai
  • Domestic FMCG — US pe depend hi nahi
  • Healthcare — India ka internal demand hai

Avoid/Watch Sectors

High Tariff Exposure
  • Solar Panels — 126% CVD duty already lagi
  • Textiles — Bangladesh se competition + tariff
  • Marine Exports — Ecuador/Vietnam se cheaper
  • Gems & Diamonds — 10% extra cost eats margin
✅ IT Services — Why So Safe? Software services pe goods tariff apply nahi hota. TCS, Infosys, Wipro ka revenue US companies se aata hai — lekin yeh "goods export" nahi, "services export" hai. GATS (General Agreement on Trade in Services) ke under services trade alag framework mein hai. Tariff war ne IT stocks ko relatively outperform kiya hai.

Tariff war ki ek badi kahani jo media miss kar rahi hai — China pe 145%+ tariff. Iska matlab? Global companies China se manufacturing shift kar rahi hain. Aur India prime beneficiary ban sakta hai.

📊 China vs India Tariff Math: China goods pe 145% tariff + India pe 10% = India 135% cheaper than China for US imports. Even with 10% tariff on India, India is massively competitive vs China. Yeh "China+1" nahi, "China replacement" hai for many categories.

💡 7. SIP Investors Ke Liye Action Plan

Tariff war ke dauraan sabse common galti — SIP band karna. Historical evidence dekho:

  • 2008 Financial Crisis: Jo investors SIP band kiye, unhone 60-70% recovery miss ki 2009-2010 mein.
  • 2020 COVID Crash: Nifty 23 March 2020 ko 7,511 tha. 18 months mein 18,000+ ho gaya. Jo ruke, unhone 2x returns liye.
  • 2022 Russia-Ukraine: 3 months mein full recovery. Jo panicke nikle, unhone loss book kiya.
🔑 Action Plan — Teen Simple Rules:

Rule 1: SIP BAND MAT KARO — market crash mein zyada units milti hain same amount mein.
Rule 2: US-export heavy funds ki exposure reduce karo — textiles, marine sector funds avoid karo.
Rule 3: Domestic consumption + IT heavy funds hold karo — HDFC Flexi Cap, Parag Parikh Flexi Cap.

📂 8. Kaun Se Mutual Funds Buy Karein?

Tariff war environment mein kaunse mutual fund categories better perform karte hain?

Safe Categories (Tariff Resistant):

  • Flexi Cap Funds: Diversified exposure — IT heavy funds safer. Parag Parikh Flexi Cap, HDFC Flexi Cap.
  • IT Sector Funds: Pure play IT — tariff immune. Franklin India Technology Fund, Tata Digital India Fund.
  • Healthcare Funds: Domestic demand + pharma. Mirae Asset Healthcare Fund.
  • FMCG Funds: Domestic consumption play. SBI Consumption Opportunities.

Avoid in Current Environment:

  • Export-heavy small cap funds with textile/marine exposure
  • Manufacturing funds with high US export dependency
  • Infrastructure funds with solar component makers

🗓️ 9. July 2026 Deadline — Teen Possible Scenarios

July 24, 2026 — Section 122 expires. Yeh critical date hai. Teen possible outcomes hain:

Scenario A — Trade Deal

Best Case — 40% probability
  • BTA (Bilateral Trade Agreement) sign ho jaata hai
  • India pe tariff 5% ya zero ho jaata hai key sectors mein
  • Nifty rally — 10-15% upside possible
  • IT, pharma, gems — all benefit

Scenario B — Extension

Base Case — 45% probability
  • Section 122 ko extend kiya jaata hai
  • 10% tariff continue rehta hai

Rupee pressure. Hedge karne ke liye: increase domestic fund allocation, reduce US-revenue dependent stocks.

❓ 10. FAQs — Aapke Saare Sawal, Jawab Ke Saath

Trump tariff ke baad India pe abhi exactly kitna tariff lag raha hai?
April 2026 mein India pe 10% flat Section 122 surcharge lag raha hai (MFN base rate ke upar). Yeh July 24, 2026 tak valid hai. Solar panels pe alag 126% CVD hai. Steel/aluminum pe 25% Section 232 pehle se tha. IT services pe ZERO tariff hai kyunki yeh goods export nahi, services export hai.
Kaunse Indian stocks tariff se safe hain?
IT services stocks (TCS, Infosys, Wipro) bilkul safe hain kyunki software services pe goods tariff nahi lagta. Generic pharma (Sun Pharma, Dr Reddy, Cipla) largely exempt hai. Domestic consumption stocks (HUL, Dabur, Apollo Hospitals) pe tariff ka seedha asar nahi kyunki inki revenue India-domestic hai.
Kya mujhe tariff war mein SIP band karni chahiye?
Bilkul nahi. Historical data dikhata hai ki har major global crisis ke 12-18 mahine baad Nifty ne strong recovery di — 2008 mein, 2020 COVID mein, 2022 Russia-Ukraine mein. SIP band karna sabse badi galti hogi kyunki crash mein zyada units milti hain. Rupee cost averaging ka yahi fayda hai.
India-US trade deal ka kya status hai?
Feb 2026 mein interim framework sign hua tha — lekin SCOTUS ne IEEPA tariff hata diya. Abhi Section 122 (10%) chal raha hai. BTA (Bilateral Trade Agreement) negotiations ongoing hain — commerce secretaries level pe meetings chal rahi hain. July 24 deadline ke baad Section 301 targeted tariffs aa sakte hain agar deal nahi hua.
China+1 strategy se India ko kya faida hoga?
China pe 145%+ tariff ki wajah se global companies India mein manufacturing shift kar rahi hain — Apple, Samsung, electronics suppliers. PLI scheme + cheap labour + English-speaking workforce + democratic governance India ko prime beneficiary banata hai. Already Apple ne India manufacturing 35% badha di hai. Long term yeh India ke liye biggest economic opportunity hai.
